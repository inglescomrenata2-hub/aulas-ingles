Real quotes from the video — click "analyse" to break down the grammar
Grey's Anatomy
"How long has it been since you've had feelings for Kate?"
Formhave/has + past participle
UseDuration — asking how long a state has existed
Key wordsHow long + has + subject + past participle
Notice 'How long has it been since…' — one of the most natural ways to ask about duration in English. Common in emotional scenes.
Modern Family
"Have you seen my engagement ring? Have you seen my fiancé?"
FormHave/Has + subject + past participle
UseExperience / recent past — asking if someone has done something at any point recently
Key wordsHave you seen / Have you heard / Have you tried
The question 'Have you seen…?' connects the past event to the present moment. She's looking for the ring RIGHT NOW — that's what makes it present perfect, not simple past.
Modern Family
"I have downloaded a to-do list. I have a photo of me and Owen."
Formhave + past participle (completed action with present result)
UseResult — action completed in the past that matters NOW
Key wordsjust, already, yet — markers of recent completion
She downloaded the list recently and NOW she has it ready. The action is done, but its result is in the present.
Grey's Anatomy
"How many have you been able to save? Three."
Formhave + been able to + infinitive
UseAccumulated experience / result up to now
Key wordsHow many have you…? How much have you…?
This is a doctor talking about his career record. He's asking about the total result across ALL his past operations — classic present perfect for accumulated experience.
Grey's Anatomy / Desperate Housewives
"I haven't talked to her in over a month. I haven't seen her in two months."
Formhave/has + NOT + past participle
UseNegative: something has NOT happened in a period of time
Key wordsin + time period, for + duration
Negative present perfect with 'in' or 'for' = emphasises the absence of an action. 'I haven't seen her for two months' = she's been gone from my life for 2 months.
Grey's Anatomy
"We've been partners for almost 5 years. That's longer than any relationship I've ever had."
Formhave/has + been + noun (continuous state) AND have/has + ever + past participle
UseDuration of ongoing state + superlative life experience
Key wordsever, for, since — key adverbs for present perfect
'I've ever had' = in my entire life experience up to now. 'We've been partners for 5 years' = started 5 years ago and STILL true today.
Desperate Housewives
"Odessa has worked here for 10 years. She has cooked and cleaned and made up your bed."
Formhas + past participle (list of actions over time)
UseDescribing what someone has done over a period, emphasising their history
Key wordsfor + years/months = duration to the present
This is emotional language — the speaker is defending Odessa by listing everything she HAS DONE. The present perfect makes the work feel ongoing and relevant to THIS moment.
